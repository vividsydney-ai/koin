# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Indonesian Gen Z (18–25), mostly students and young workers. They use their phone throughout the day in short bursts. They are skeptical of financial advice but curious about investing because friends or family talk about it. Many have tried Instagram/TikTok finance content but don't trust it. They want to learn without feeling talked down to.

## Product Purpose

Koin makes financial literacy sticky by combining Duolingo-style micro-lessons with a paper trading sandbox. Every lesson cites a Tier 1 Indonesian source (OJK, BI, IDX) so trust is the product. The goal is not just information transfer — it's behavior change: save first, avoid scams, understand inflation, and eventually invest responsibly.

## Brand Personality

- **Trustworthy but not boring**: every claim is sourced, but the tone is conversational.
- **Playfully Indonesian**: local examples (mie ayam, GoPay, warung) beat generic finance metaphors.
- **Confidently simple**: complex concepts are explained in one breath, not ten.
- **Encouraging**: mistakes are learning moments, not failures.

## Anti-references

- Generic fintech SaaS templates (navy + gold, hero metrics, gradient buttons).
- Overly gamified ed-apps with cartoon mascots and infantilizing copy.
- Long-form blog-style lessons that feel like homework.
- Glassmorphism, gradient text, and ghost-card shadows.
- Cream/sand default "warmth" backgrounds.

## Design Principles

1. **Source before opinion**: every lesson shows its source card before the quiz.
2. **Bite-sized or nothing**: one concept per screen; no scrolling through walls of text.
3. **Local examples first**: abstract concepts land through Indonesian daily life.
4. **Motion with meaning**: animations explain, not decorate.
5. **Trust through restraint**: no dark patterns, no fake urgency, no hidden risk.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA target.
- Reduced-motion fallback for all animations.
- Minimum 44px touch targets.
- Clear contrast on all text, including muted labels.
